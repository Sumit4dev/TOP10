Popular Artifacts of TOP10 Museums
The Egyptian mummy housed in the Albert Hall Museum in Jaipur is one of the museum's most intriguing and famous exhibits. This mummy, named Tutu, was brought to Jaipur in the late 19th century from Egypt and is one of the very few Egyptian mummies preserved and displayed in India. Tutu was believed to be a member of an ancient priestly family, and her presence in the museum adds an air of mystery and fascination, drawing both locals and tourists. Encased in a glass chamber within the museum’s dimly lit hall, Tutu’s mummy is preserved with remarkable detail. Visitors can observe her intricate wrappings and the symbols painted on her sarcophagus, which reflect ancient Egyptian beliefs about life, death, and the afterlife. The display also includes information on Egyptian mummification processes, which were used to preserve bodies for the afterlife—a significant part of ancient Egyptian religion and culture.
