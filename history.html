Albert Hall
Plaque for Samuel Swinton Jacob at Albert Hall Museum
The Albert Hall Museum, a prominent museum in Jaipur, Rajasthan, India, is celebrated for its Indo-Saracenic architecture and expansive collection of art and artifacts. Maharaja Ram Singh initially envisioned the building as a town hall, but his successor, Maharaja Madho Singh II, later repurposed it into a museum as part of the Ram Nivas Garden development. This decision aligned with the intention to preserve and showcase Jaipur's artistic heritage. Designed by British architect Samuel Swinton Jacob, the structure’s foundation stone was laid on February 6, 1876, during the visit of Prince Albert Edward later King Edward VII), giving the museum its name. The Albert Hall officially opened its doors as a public museum in 1887.
Colonel Thomas H. Hendley, who curated the museum’s collection, had previously gathered a variety of art and crafts pieces for an exhibition in Jaipur in 1883, forming the core of what would become the museum’s collection. His cataloged inventory of these items remains an important reference for the museum to this day. Hendley also pioneered new practices in visitor engagement by appointing guides to offer tours and setting aside Fridays specifically for female visitors, a significant consideration given the region's purdah tradition. These forward-thinking measures established Albert Hall as a model museum, influencing other institutions worldwide.
