Anokhi Musuem Of Jaipur
Anokhi Museum Of Handprinting in Jaipur
The Anokhi Museum of Hand Printing was founded by Rachel Bracken-Singh and her husband, Pritam Singh. In the late 1970s, Pritam’s father, John Singh, acquired the neglected Chanwar Palkiwalon ki Haveli mansion and began a three-year renovation in 1989. Under the guidance of architects Nimish Patel and Parul Zaveri from Abhikram, the mansion was meticulously restored to its former grandeur. This preservation project, which showcased the effectiveness of using indigenous materials, traditional construction methods, and skilled local artisans, received a UNESCO award for 'Cultural Heritage Conservation' in 2000.
After the restoration was completed, the future use of the haveli remained undecided. Eventually, Rachel and Pritam decided to establish a museum dedicated to hand block printing. To achieve this, structural adaptations were made to transform the 16th-century haveli into a contemporary space suitable for displaying delicate textiles. Additionally, the Anokhi Archives required upgraded storage facilities and further research. This preparation led to the museum’s opening exhibition, Print Progress – Innovation & Revival 1970-2005.
Sixteen years later the work continues. Museum practices are continually refined, and an ongoing research programme unearths more tantalising bits of history as new information on people and places come to light. Our neighbourhood is also key. AMHP strives to educate every student in Amber, enhancing their knowledge and pride in their esteemed textile heritage. The museum also welcomes our neighbours and local craftsmen to experience a living history museum first hand.
